About Me

I am a Postdoctoral Research Fellow at the Research Center for Applied Sciences, Academia Sinica, specializing in quantum mechanics-based computational methods including density functional theory (DFT) and semi-empirical pseudopotential methods (SEPM). My research bridges theoretical modeling with applications in novel quantum materials, focusing on their electronic and optical properties. I hold a PhD in Physics from the University of Texas at Austin, where I developed new computational methods for studying excitonic effects in two-dimensional materials.

Under the supervision of Prof. Yia-Chung Chang, my current work focuses on developing advanced computational frameworks to understand and predict material properties at quantum scales, with particular emphasis on exciton physics in 2D systems and nanostructured materials.
