Dr. Raj Kumar Paudel

Postdoctoral Research Fellow in Computational Physics

About Me

I am a Postdoctoral Research Fellow at Research Center for Applied Sciences, Academia Sinica specializing in quantum mechanics based methods such as density functional theory and semi-empirical pseudopotential methods. My work bridges theoretical modeling with applications in novel quantum materials and their electronic and optical properties. I hold a PhD in Physics from the University of Texas at Austin, where I developed new computational methods for studying excitonic effects in two-dimensional materials.

Research Focus

My research focuses on developing and applying computational methods to understand electronic and optical properties of quantum materials. Key areas include:

Method Development
  • Semi-Empirical Pseudopotential Method (SEPM)
  • Exciton modeling approaches
  • Hybrid DFT methods
Material Systems
  • 2D materials (TMDCs, graphene)
  • Fullerenes (C₆₀ and derivatives)
  • Van der Waals heterostructures
