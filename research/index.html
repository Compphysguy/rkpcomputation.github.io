Computational Method

Semi-Empirical Pseudopotential Method

We've developed a semi-empirical pseudopotential (SEPM) approach that efficiently calculates electronic structures of nanomaterials by combining plane waves with B-spline basis functions. This method is parameterized to accurately reproduce DFT results while being computationally efficient.

  • Combines 2D plane waves with B-spline functions
  • Incorporates local and non-local terms
  • Parameterized using DFT benchmarks
Quantum Phenomena

Exciton Physics in 2D Materials

Our research focuses on exciton binding energies and dynamics in transition metal dichalcogenides (TMDCs). We develop theoretical frameworks to model these quantum phenomena in monolayer, bilayer, and twisted bilayer systems.

Graphene and Nanoribbons

Our SEPM approach has been successfully applied to study the electronic properties of graphene and armchair graphene nanoribbons (aGNRs). The method accurately captures band structures and edge effects that are crucial for nanoscale electronic applications.
